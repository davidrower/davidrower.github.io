I'm a classical nerd.

Along with my physics, I've always enjoyed music. I've played piano most of my life, and cello for a little less time. At the beginning of undergrad, I assembled a casual string quartet to play through some standard repertoire (we got stuck on Borodin and Mendelssohn). Recently, I have been doing the same with the piano trio repertoire (currently my favorite chamber ensemble, stuck on Schubert and Mendelssohn).

I'm a big Western music history nerd as well. In case you need a fun fact, here's a gem: Tchaikovsky wrote an elegy trio to Nikolai Rubinstein, and Rachmaninoff wrote an elegy trio to Tchaikovsky, but there's an obscure and sparsely recorded Russian composer, Alexander Goldenweiser, who wrote an elegy trio for Rachmaninoff, completing the tragic trio of elegy trios. I wouldn't be surprised if this goes deeper as well.

I have more Rachmaninoff days than anything else. I've also been stuck on Medtner this past year.
