Computational Biophysics

I am currently working several projects with Dr. Paul Atzberger, leader of an applied mathematics research group at UCSB. We are studying coarse grained models for lipid bilayer membranes and hydrodynamics. (side note - getting involved in this group was my first serious exposure to research and computing, and I am immensely thankful for the mentorship and motivation Dr. Atzberger has provided me).

For more details on my work, take a look at the (recently posted) arXiv article, or a recent talk I gave at the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics Conference on Life Sciences (SIAM LS) 2018.

Autonomous Vehicles

I involved myself in a more applied research group in the ECE department of UCSB. I explored this for around a quarter, and in that time performed some validation simulations of road capacity models for heterogenous traffic flows. The source code and writeup for my simulations can be found here.
