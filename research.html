Material Defects in Superconducting Circuits

Superconducting circuits have become valuable tools for a variety of applications - from particle detectors and nanoscale magnetometers to quantum information processors and quantum simulators. However, these circuits are plagued by material defects that hinder their functionality by leaking away energy and information.

I am developing tools to explore these defects, which I hope will prove useful in aiding our microscopic understanding of them and inform the fabrication of the next generation of superconducting circuits. This is in the context of pursuing a PhD in condensed matter experimental physics at MIT in the groups of Will Oliver and Riccardo Comin.
