Motivation

I was introduced to Python a few years ago. Since then, I've become addicted to programming. It's incredibly enabling. I can realize nearly any idea, and I can explore nearly any branch of physics I want via knowing a model and some numerical analysis. Some fun little projects I've done are on my github. When I have a choice, I use Python. However, I do what I need to do to survive (Bash, C++, Mathematica, Matlab, JavaScript, Google APIs, etc).

Scientific Development

I have used Python for numerous projects with applications everywhere from differential geometry calculations to autonomous vehicle interactions. I feel extremely comfortable with the language, and I am excited to bring this to a condensed matter experiment (CMX) lab in grad school.

I conduct research and perform development work for projects with Dr. Paul Atzberger (mentioned on the research page) and my current job with Toyon Research Corporation. My work has naturally led me to utilize much Python, C++, Matlab, Bash, with Git and SVN for version control.

I spent some time interning at OpenEye Scientific Software. My project concerned several numerical optimization techniques, in addition to some other interesting numerical experiments. Most of my work utilized C++ and Python.
